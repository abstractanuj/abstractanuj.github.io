What the heck is an Atom?

Mar 06, 2024 • #meta, #physics
0. The Atom is the smallest unit of matter.
They are solid but they also have smaller subatomic particles inside them.
There is empty space between these subatomic particles and it is of a considerable amount.

1. Electrons are negatively charged particles that lie in an orbital around the nucleus, in their respective "shells".

Note: Orbit is different from an orbital.
Orbit is a pathway defined mathematically.
Whereas an orbital is a 3-Dimensional region (let's call it an electron cloud) around the nucleus within which we can find elections 90% of the time. It's more like an abstract probability space than an actual 3D sphere. 

Why don't electrons fall into the nucleus?
Since they are negatively charged and protons are positively charged they must attract eachother and therefore electrons must fall into the nucleus. What's stopping them?
This could have been the case if the electrons were orbiting around the nucleus.
a. Electrons move very fast, in random directions inside their electron cloud.
b. Heisenberg Uncertainty Principle — You can never know where a particle is and where it's going at the same time.
Say, if hypothetically the electron must exist inside the nucleus then it must be going way par the speed of light. Which is not possible.

2. Nucleus is a place where neutron and proton lie closely packed together.
Neutron acts like a glue, sticking together protons and itself.
Why don't protons repel eachother inside the nucleus? b/c of the neutron binding them together, as well as b/c of the fact that the energy with which protons repel eachother is very less as compared to electron-electron repulsion. They do try to repel eachother all the time but without any success remain together.

3. Protons are positively charged particles.

4. Neutrons are neutral, i.e. they don't have any charge.

5. Charge is apparently just a name given to the effect of an electron, proton and neutron. Talking about charge is useless unless we specify what kind of charge. i.e. positive charge and or negative charge.

In an atom -- protons carry postive charge. Meaning they must be repelling eachother inside the nucleus, on the other hand, the electrons orbiting in the shells must also be trying to repel eachother.

• Protons do not participate when an atom tries to bond with another atom -- it's the electrons that actively participate.
